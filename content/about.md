## Some of my core opinions:
Even though I am always open for dialogue, I believe that there are some opinions that are harder to shake out:

### Freedom of information
All the information that is relevant to the public should be open to the public. Be that documents from the government or even companies. The idea of keeping contracts hidden or doing things behind closed doors is generally bad for the average person.

And openness also means that the data is accessible. Accessibility means
1. All the data can be found by a person. If the data is available, but cannot be found, then it isn't open.
2. The data has to be readable by any person. This is in terms of people with disabilities, but also in terms of any other restriction like age-verification.
3. The data cannot be hard to obtain. This means that you do not have to create an account and give up your personal information or you have to drive 300 kilometers to get this data.

This is obviously not possible for everything, but especially governments should strive to achieve openness.

### Copyright and digital rights
Unfortunatly the current copyright system that was kinda accepted worldwide does not work with the current state of the world and the internet.
We live in an age that has a rapid flow of information and then we have copyright laws that allows companies to build hostile DRM to the consumer
to prevent them from using that product in the way they want. With licenses and the behaviour that the customer does not actually own the stuff they have bought we spend more on basic commodities as well as leave our freedom behind.

> You'll own nothing and be happy
> 
> ---World Economic Forum

To ensure that we'll own nothing and won't be happy, we are required to connect to remote servers to "activate our product" and have to give our entire identity to the provider whilst also accepting that we sell our soul to ensure that the interests of the stakeholders are protected.\
This is not done to protect us or to help us. This is used against us.

That's why my website is licensed under the CC BY-SA 4.0 license to ensure that everyone has access to my content (however insignificant my content actually is).

### AI and other big tech shenanigans
I believe that AI, like anything else, can be a good tools that has it's own niche. And I think that the idea of AI is actually quite cool and when it is being used in a way that actually makes sense, it is awesome. But this is unfortunately not reality.

We currently have companies that invest in trillions of dollars into this technology and build data centres that are actually harmful to the environment in terms of energy usage, sound pollution and other problems. 
It is also embedded into everything that can be used with an internet connection whilst it was trained on material that was illegaly obtained with the companies, despite that, just being more and more secretive and less open with their models. 
It is also a great way for companies to spy even more on their users as all of the data has to be sent to an external server.

There are also the people that do not understand what they are dealing with and just investing in AI because they know that it is a trend.
They require AI to be used in every part of the company and actually believe that this tool can replace their workers. Which is idiotic as AI is not good at all in terms of critical thinking. Similarly to any other program on the computer, it just does what it was trained to do.

In this regard I am saying that all the texts and code that is written on this site, is **not written by AI** and entirely human.
